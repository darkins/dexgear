## What It Is

The SM58 is a dynamic cardioid microphone with a frequency response of 50 Hz–15 kHz and a characteristic presence peak around 5 kHz that adds vocal intelligibility and cuts through a mix. It is the standard for live vocal performance worldwide and a reliable studio workhorse for recording vocals, guitars, and instrument sources.

## Key Characteristics

<ul class="space-y-4 list-disc pl-6">
  <li><strong>Dynamic element:</strong> no phantom power required — safe to plug in with MOTU's +48V phantom power switched off on input 1. Never hot-plug while phantom is on (audible thump).</li>
  <li><strong>Cardioid polar pattern:</strong> rejects sound from the rear and sides. Minimises monitor bleed, room noise, and phase buildup from other open sources in the room.</li>
  <li><strong>Integrated shock mount:</strong> a pneumatic shock mount system inside the capsule housing isolates the element from handling noise and stand vibration.</li>
  <li><strong>Ball grille as pop filter:</strong> the close-mesh ball grille reduces plosive transients (P, B sounds) and wind noise. No separate pop filter needed for casual use, though a studio pop filter is recommended for precision vocal tracking.</li>
  <li><strong>Proximity effect:</strong> bass response increases when singing very close to the capsule (&lt;1 inch). This can be used musically for a warm, intimate sound, or cut with the CueMix FX hi-pass EQ at 80–100 Hz to clean it up.</li>
</ul>

## Gain Staging at the MOTU

1. Connect SM58 via XLR to **MOTU input 1** (the mic/instrument combo jack).
2. Ensure **phantom power (+48V) is OFF** on input 1 — the SM58 is a dynamic mic and does not need it.
3. Start with input gain at approximately **50–60%** of the physical knob range.
4. Have the vocalist do a full-volume test run. Monitor the input level in **CueMix FX**.
5. Aim for peaks in the **upper yellow zone** (around −6 dBFS). Never allow the signal to clip into the red.
6. Dynamic mics like the SM58 output a relatively low signal — don't be afraid to push the preamp gain higher than you might expect.

## Mic Technique

<ul class="space-y-4 list-disc pl-6">
  <li><strong>Distance:</strong> 1–2 inches from the capsule for studio clarity with a tight, present sound. Move further away (4–6 inches) for more room air and a natural sound, at the cost of more ambient noise pickup.</li>
  <li><strong>On-axis:</strong> pointing the capsule directly at the mouth provides maximum output and presence peak. Slight off-axis (15–30°) reduces sibilance if the vocalist has harsh 's' sounds.</li>
  <li><strong>Cup technique (live performance):</strong> cupping the ball grille with the hand causes feedback buildup by disrupting the polar pattern — avoid in studio, or use deliberately for a lo-fi effect.</li>
</ul>

## Recording Chain in Ableton

**Standard vocal chain:**

1. SM58 → MOTU input 1 → Ableton Audio Track (set MIDI From to MOTU In 1/2, Audio input to Ch 1 Mono)
2. CueMix FX: apply MOTU's **Modeled EQ** in hardware — hi-pass at 80 Hz to remove rumble and proximity effect buildup
3. CueMix FX: apply MOTU's **LA-2A Leveler** in hardware — moderate Peak Reduction for transparent gain control
4. In Ableton post-recording: **EQ Eight** (refine hi-pass, tame any boxiness at 300–500 Hz, add +2 dB presence at 3–5 kHz) → **Compressor** → **Reverb Send** (Return track)

## Dual Recording Setup

Record both a clean and processed vocal simultaneously for maximum flexibility:

- **Track A (clean):** SM58 → MOTU input 1 → Ableton Audio Track 1. Dry, unprocessed signal — ideal for heavy post-production.
- **Track B (VT-3 processed):** SM58 → VT-3 → MOTU inputs 3/4 → Ableton Audio Track 2. The VT-3 character printed as audio.

Set both Audio Tracks to record-enabled before pressing Record. Blend or choose in the mix. The clean track provides an insurance policy — any VT-3 character choice can always be revisited in the arrangement.
