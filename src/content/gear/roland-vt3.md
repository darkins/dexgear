## Core Controls

<ul class="space-y-4 list-disc pl-6">
  <li><strong>Pitch slider:</strong> large, central slider that shifts vocal pitch up or down in real time — from a full octave down to an octave up.</li>
  <li><strong>Formant slider:</strong> changes the vocal tract character independently of pitch — shift the formant up to sound smaller/higher, down to sound larger/lower. Combine with Pitch for completely transformed voice textures.</li>
  <li><strong>Reverb slider:</strong> built-in reverb depth for the output signal — adds space and dimension without needing a separate effects unit.</li>
  <li><strong>Mix slider:</strong> wet/dry ratio between the processed signal and the direct input. Useful for subtle enhancement (Mix low) or full transformation (Mix at max).</li>
</ul>

## Voice Characters

<ul class="space-y-4 list-disc pl-6">
  <li><strong>Vocoder (VP-330 style):</strong> classic vocoder effect that speech-modulates a carrier. No keyboard required — the VT-3 generates an internal carrier. Connect the Launchkey 37's MIDI Out to the VT-3's USB MIDI input to play vocoder pitch musically in real time.</li>
  <li><strong>Auto-Pitch (Classic):</strong> melodic pitch correction with a natural-sounding feel — subtle tuning assistance with a musical character.</li>
  <li><strong>Auto-Pitch (Hard):</strong> stepped, quantised pitch tuning for the well-known hard pitch correction sound.</li>
  <li><strong>Scatter:</strong> glitchy, fragmented, broken-sounding effect — the voice is scattered across time in short repeating bursts.</li>
  <li><strong>Megaphone:</strong> narrow bandwidth, distorted telephone/bullhorn character.</li>
  <li><strong>Radio:</strong> AM-style radio signal simulation with noise and bandwidth limiting.</li>
  <li><strong>Robot:</strong> monotone robotic pitched voice — classic science fiction vocal character. Pitch is set by the Pitch slider.</li>
  <li><strong>Synth Lead:</strong> transforms the voice into a synthesizer lead tone, tracking vocal pitch.</li>
  <li><strong>Synth Bass:</strong> sub-octave synth bass derived from the vocal input — speak or sing to generate bass lines.</li>
</ul>

## 6 User Presets

Store up to 6 complete snapshots (voice character + Pitch + Formant + Reverb + Mix settings) to the VT-3's internal memory. Recall any preset in one button press during a performance. Presets can also be recalled via MIDI Program Change messages from Ableton or a MIDI footswitch for hands-free switching.

## Built-in USB Audio Interface

The VT-3 functions as a secondary audio interface when connected via USB:

- The processed vocal output appears as a separate USB audio stream — record it directly into Ableton as a clean digital take alongside the analog feed into MOTU inputs 3/4.
- **Loopback recording** routes the final processed signal back into the DAW without any additional cables — useful for capturing the VT-3 output in isolation for mixing separately.

## MIDI Control (v1.1+)

<ul class="space-y-4 list-disc pl-6">
  <li><strong>Vocoder pitch via USB MIDI:</strong> connect the Launchkey 37 MIDI Out → VT-3 USB MIDI In (or MOTU MIDI Out → VT-3 MIDI In). Notes played on the keyboard set the vocoder's carrier pitch in real time — play a melody and speak into the SM58 for polyphonic-sounding vocoder phrases.</li>
  <li><strong>Control Change:</strong> automate VT-3 Pitch, Formant, Reverb, and Mix parameters from Ableton via MIDI CC for repeatable, automated vocal effects.</li>
  <li><strong>Program Change:</strong> switch between the 6 user presets from an Ableton Clip's MIDI output, a MIDI footswitch, or the Launchkey 37's Custom Mode pads.</li>
</ul>

## Signal Chain

**Live/studio routing:**

1. **SM58 (XLR)** → VT-3 XLR/TRS combo input (phantom power OFF — SM58 is dynamic).
2. **VT-3 monitor output** → performer's headphones or stage monitor for real-time wet/dry feedback without going through the computer.
3. **VT-3 stereo line output** → MOTU inputs 3/4 (line level — set MOTU gain to 12 o'clock).
4. **VT-3 USB** → computer for separate digital stream if dual-capture is desired.

**Dual recording for maximum flexibility:**

- **Audio Track A (clean):** SM58 → MOTU input 1 → Ableton Audio Track. Dry, unprocessed signal preserved for production decisions later.
- **Audio Track B (VT-3):** VT-3 stereo → MOTU inputs 3/4 → Ableton Audio Track. The VT-3 character captured as printed audio.

Record both simultaneously. Blend or choose in the mix based on creative needs.

## Live Performance Tips

<ul class="space-y-4 list-disc pl-6">
  <li>Add a <strong>footswitch</strong> (optional 1/4-inch TRS) to the VT-3's footswitch input for hands-free bypass and preset recall while performing.</li>
  <li>Assign the Launchkey 37's Custom Mode pads to MIDI Program Change 1–6 to recall VT-3 presets from a keyboard pad without reaching for the unit.</li>
  <li>For vocoder with musical pitch: Launchkey 37 MIDI Out → VT-3 USB MIDI In. Play chords or melodies on the Launchkey while speaking into the SM58 for melodic vocoder phrases.</li>
  <li>All downstream Ableton processing (EQ, compression, reverb) remains independent — route the MOTU input signal through the same vocal chain as the dry SM58 signal.</li>
</ul>
