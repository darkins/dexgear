## What It Is

The HS7 is a bi-amplified studio reference monitor featuring a 6.5-inch woofer and a 1-inch tweeter. The design philosophy is accuracy: a flat, uncoloured frequency response intended to expose problems in a mix rather than make it sound better than it is. The white woofer cone is iconic. When mixes translate well on HS7s, they translate well everywhere.

- **LF amplifier:** 60W
- **HF amplifier:** 35W
- **Frequency response:** 43 Hz – 30 kHz

## Rear-Panel Room Adjustments

<ul class="space-y-4 list-disc pl-6">
  <li><strong>Room Control (low shelf):</strong> switchable to −2 dB or −4 dB at 500 Hz. Use when the monitors are placed close to a rear wall — room boundary reinforcement causes bass buildup that this switch compensates for. If the speakers are free-standing away from walls, leave it at 0 dB (flat).</li>
  <li><strong>High Trim:</strong> switchable to −2 dB, flat (0 dB), or +2 dB at 2 kHz. Compensates for room acoustics that affect high-mid presence. Most treated rooms: flat. Rooms with heavy absorption: +2 dB. Very bright/reflective rooms: −2 dB.</li>
</ul>

## Connection to MOTU

1. Run balanced cables from **MOTU Main Outputs (channels 1/2)** to the HS7 left and right inputs.
2. Use **TRS-to-TRS** cables if the MOTU output jacks and HS7 input jacks are both TRS (¼-inch). Use **TRS-to-XLR** if the HS7 inputs are XLR — either works, both are balanced for maximum noise rejection.
3. Set monitoring volume via the **VOLUME knob on the HS7 rear panel** to a comfortable reference level, then control session levels inside CueMix FX or Ableton's Master fader. Avoid using just the MOTU output as a volume control — set a fixed hardware level on the HS7 and manage levels in software.

## Speaker Placement Essentials

<ul class="space-y-4 list-disc pl-6">
  <li><strong>Ear height:</strong> the tweeter should be at ear level when seated at the mix position. Adjust stand height accordingly.</li>
  <li><strong>Equilateral triangle:</strong> left speaker, right speaker, and listener position should all be roughly equidistant (typically 3–4 ft in a home studio). This ensures the stereo image is symmetrical and the listening position is in the optimal sweet spot.</li>
  <li><strong>Toe-in angle:</strong> angle the speakers inward approximately 30° toward the listening position. Too little toe-in widens the stereo image but reduces precision; too much narrows it.</li>
  <li><strong>Wall distance:</strong> at least 2 ft from side walls and 3 ft from the rear wall. The closer a speaker is to a wall, the more bass reinforcement it receives. Use the Room Control switch if rear-wall placement is unavoidable.</li>
</ul>

## Monitoring Practices

<ul class="space-y-4 list-disc pl-6">
  <li><strong>Reference level:</strong> mix primarily at a moderate, consistent volume — approximately 85 dBSPL at the mix position. This level provides consistent hearing physiology (the equal-loudness curves flatten) and helps protect hearing during long sessions.</li>
  <li><strong>Low-volume check:</strong> regularly flip to conversation volume (~70 dBSPL). If the mix still sounds balanced at low volume, it will translate well on earbuds, laptop speakers, and car stereos. If the bass disappears entirely, it may be over-represented at reference level.</li>
  <li><strong>Mono check:</strong> use the X-Y Lissajous plot in CueMix FX Analysis to verify stereo correlation. A diagonal line means the signal is phase-coherent and mono-compatible. A scattered blob suggests wide stereo; correlated horizontal/vertical components suggest phase issues.</li>
  <li><strong>Headphone cross-check:</strong> after a session on the HS7s, listen to the mix on headphones connected to the MOTU phones output. Headphones reveal different balance issues — often catching blend problems the room's acoustic reflections may mask.</li>
</ul>

## Integration in the Studio Chain

The HS7 monitors are the final output destination of everything in the studio:

- All production, mixing, and monitoring passes through the HS7 pair.
- The MOTU outputs levels as determined by CueMix FX Mix 1 (the main output bus).
- The Hydrasynth, SM58, and VT-3 all ultimately feed through the MOTU main outputs to the HS7s.
- When recording with live monitoring (e.g., vocalist tracking), switch monitoring to headphones via the MOTU Phones output to avoid feedback loops.
